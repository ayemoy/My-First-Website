About Me




Moshe (Nosy) Nussbaum, born on October 11, 1953, is an Israeli journalist, commentator on police affairs and internal security, who has been a reporter for police affairs at the news company since its establishment in 1993. He is the recipient of the Lifetime Achievement Award for Journalism (2022). Nussbaum was born and raised in Netanya. He did his military service in the 202nd Battalion of the Parachute Brigade, and during the Yom Kippur War, while he was in an officers' course, he was converted to armor, and there he finished his service as a tank company Sgt. and went on to a master's degree in communication. In July 2022, Nussbaum revealed that his doctors estimate that he is suffering from ALS - an incurable muscular dystrophy disease, and announced his intention to reduce his appearances on news releases.
